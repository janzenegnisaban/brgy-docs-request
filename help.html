Help & FAQ

Find answers to common questions about requesting barangay documents.

Getting Started

Do I need an account to submit a request?

No. You can submit a request as a guest. You will receive a tracking number to check your request status. Creating an account lets you manage all your requests in one dashboard and claim any previous guest requests.

What documents can I request?

You can request: Barangay Clearance, Certificate of Indigency, Certificate of Residency, Business Permit Clearance, Community Tax Certificate, and other barangay-issued documents.

What information do I need to provide?

Full name, birthdate, complete address, email, and phone number. You may also need to upload a valid ID, proof of residency, or other documents depending on the document type.

Tracking & Status

How do I track my request?

Go to the Track Request page and enter your tracking number (e.g. BRG-2026-001234). You can also use the link sent in your confirmation.

What do the statuses mean?

Pending – Your request is under review. For Printing – Document is being prepared. For Signing – Awaiting barangay captain’s signature. Ready – Document is ready for pickup or download. Completed – Request fulfilled. Rejected – Request was not approved.

How long does processing take?

Typically 3–5 business days. You will be notified when your document is ready. You can track status anytime using your tracking number.

Downloads & Pickup

When can I download my document?

When the status is Ready or Completed, a “Download” button will appear on your request. You can print the document or save it as PDF from the print dialog.

Can I still pick up a physical copy?

Yes. You may pick up the signed document at the barangay office during office hours (Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM–5:00 PM). Bring a valid ID.

Duplicate Requests & Limits

Why am I seeing a duplicate request warning?

If you already have a pending request for the same document type (within the last 30 days) with the same name and contact details, the system will warn you. You can view the existing request or choose to submit anyway if needed.

Are there limits on how many requests I can submit?

To prevent abuse, there is a limit of 3 requests per email per day and 5 requests per day from the same device. Contact the barangay office if you have a special need.

Contact

For further assistance, visit the Barangay New Cabalan office or contact us during office hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
